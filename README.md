**Qubit**
A qubit is the basic unit of quantum information in quantum computing, analogous to a classical bit in traditional computing. 

**Basic Properties**

_Superposition:_ 
                Unlike a classical bit, which can be either 0 or 1, a qubit can exist in a super position of both states simultaneously. This means it can be 0, 1 or any quantum combinations of two.

_Entanglement_:
                 Qubits can be entangled , meaning the state of one qubit is directly related to the state of another, no matter ho far apart they are. This property allows qunatum computers to perform complex calculations more efficiently than classical computers.

_Measurement_:
                When a qubit is measured, it collapses from its superposition state to one of the basis states (either |0> or |1>) with probabilities determined by |Alpha|^2 and |Beta|^2 

_Quantum States:_
                  In quantum computing we work with the quantum analogue of classical bits. These are called qubits (quantum bits), and they have two fundamental basis states:
|0>
|1>
